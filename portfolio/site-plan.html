About Me

My name is Matthew Foushee. I am 22 years old. I am engaged and will be married shortly. I have been programming now for 10 years finding my love of programming in 6th grade in a lego robotics unit of some class. The following year I joined my schools lego robotics team where I programmed the robot using the block programming that gets provided. I did that for only one year where starting in 9th grade I joined my school’s FTC team making actual robots out of metal and motors programming the robot in Java. I fell in love with it and taught myself(with help from youtube) how to program. My life for the next 3 years was: school, Robotics, home, program personal project, repeat

FTC robotics

FTC robotics is the First Tech Challenge. It's an official world wide league that releases a yearly competition with unique rules and gameplay that each team needs to design a robot to compete in.

Personal Project Journey

I started many many many projects and not finishing most of them have only a few saved to Github or the files saved. I started in Java because that was the language I was learning for robotics and started immediately making graphical type things. I learned all sorts of things involving the graphics pipeline and by senior year I switched to c++, the freedom I felt it gave me was immense.

Why I switched to C++

For the first 3 years of my programming life I used Java. It was familiar and read like English, once you knew how to program. After I made my own custom graphics renderer without any help of the GPU(dumb idea) I blamed the language for being slow because I didn't know otherwise. This caused me to look into what “real” graphics engines were made with and the resounding answer is c++. In my learning journey though I did learn that you NEED to use the graphics card to render things fast/optimally so I started doing that too but now in a new language.
